Theory -- Simple Pendulum

A simple pendulum consists of a mass (bob) suspended from a fixed point by a string. For small angles (less than 15 degrees), the motion is approximately simple harmonic.

The period depends only on length and gravity -- not on mass or amplitude (for small angles). Doubling the length increases the period by a factor of the square root of 2. On the Moon (g = 1.6 m/s squared), a 1m pendulum has T = 5s compared to 2s on Earth.

Energy oscillates between kinetic (maximum at center) and potential (maximum at extremes). Total mechanical energy is conserved in the absence of friction.
