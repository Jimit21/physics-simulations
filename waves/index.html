Wave Superposition & Interference

✅

Constructive Interference

When two waves are in phase (phase difference = 0° or 360°), their amplitudes add up: Ares = A₁ + A₂. The resultant wave is larger than either individual wave.

❌

Destructive Interference

When two equal waves are out of phase (phase difference = 180°), they cancel completely: Ares = 0. This is the principle behind noise-cancelling headphones!

🔔

Beats

When two waves have slightly different frequencies, the resultant amplitude oscillates at the beat frequency: fbeat = |f₁ − f₂|. Used to tune musical instruments!
