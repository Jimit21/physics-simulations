Theory -- Electric Fields

An electric field is created by electric charges and exerts a force on other charges in the region. Field lines point away from positive charges and toward negative charges.

Field strength (E) is inversely proportional to r squared -- it weakens with distance. Between a positive and negative charge (dipole), field lines form characteristic curves from positive to negative.

Superposition principle: The total field is the vector sum of fields from each charge. Add multiple charges and observe how the net field changes direction and magnitude everywhere.
