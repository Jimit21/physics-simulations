What Is the Photoelectric Effect?

The Discovery

When light of sufficient frequency hits a metal surface, electrons are ejected. Albert Einstein explained this in 1905 using the concept of light quanta (photons), winning him the Nobel Prize in Physics.

The Key Rule

Only frequency determines if electrons are emitted (not intensity). Intensity only controls how many electrons come out per second (the current).

The Formula

KEmax = hν − φ
where h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s (Planck's constant), ν = frequency of light, φ = work function of metal.
